History:

In India, the game of Ludo first appeared around 3300 BC. This timeless game is simple to learn and entertaining for both kids and adults.

Know the play:

Ludo is traditionally a board game. It consists of four sections of different colors:
  • Blue
  • Yellow
  • Red
  • Green

The first colored space outside is starting position leading to a clockwise path to the final position lead to centre home triangle.

Game is usually played with 2 to 4 people. Each take turns trowing the die. Game starts when one gets highest number (=6).
The Goal is to move all four paws in the home triangle.

If a person gets six: the player is eligible for another turn to roll the die. And can move the pawn twice in same turn.
Be Careful! If the player gets three 6 in a row, the player loses his turn.

Tips and strategies:


Few tips:
  • Know your game: It is good to know the your strength, that is, you wish to move pawns in a group, so more power or individually to take your time and win strategically.
  • Try to get as many sixes as possible: It is good to have all your pawns out for better strategy.
  • Capture pawn of other players: Ludo is a game of strategy and involves not only simple winning but capturing other pawns to send them back home to clear your way to win.
  • The colored points in the board are safe spaces. So no player can cut the pawn there and send it back home.

Note:

Everyone has their own strategies and way to win. Its a game of mind and luck. Play it. Learn from experience and implement new strategies.
