BTS💜


Bangtan Sonyeondan (Bangtan Boys or Beyond The Scene), better known as BTS, is a South Korean boy band that was founded in 2010.
Their music addresses issues such as mental health, difficulties associated with adolescence and growing up, loss, the path to self-love, individualism, and the effects of fame and recognition.
BTS made their Big Hit Entertainment debut in 2013 with the release of the single album 2 Cool 4 School.
Breaking numerous world records, BTS has distinguished themselves as "21st century Pop Icons."

How did the group formed?

Bang Si-Hyuk, a well-known songwriter and music producer who founded his own company, Big Hit Entertainment, in 2005, is the creator of BTS.
He started gathering musicians in 2010 for a band he was calling the Bangtan Sonyeondan (also known as the BTS).

Army


The fandom is called ARMY [“Adorable Representative MC for Youth”].
The relationship between BTS and ARMY, according to South Korean author Jiyoung Lee, is:
a mutual exchange between artists and their fans that aims to extending the band's message of positivity into the world rather than just ensure the band's primacy.

Why name ARMY?

BTS singer RM said during an appearance on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon that the fandom was almost called "The Bells" due to the similarity between the first syllables of the band's name (bang) and the word "bell" in Korean.
There are two meanings behind the name ARMY, which stands for more than just being a fan of BTS. The abbreviation ARMY, which stands for "Adorable Representative M.C. For Youth," literally means "army."
Bangtan Sonyeondan, the Korean name of BTS, translates to "Bulletproof Boy Scouts" in English. They refer to their followers as their "ARMY," and BTS is the armour that shields them, illustrating the band's commitment to its ARMY.

I 💜 you:

Kim Taehyung came up with the word 'Borahae', during BTS' third muster in November, 2016.
It was combination of of two words: 'Bora', which means purple and 'Saranghae', which means love.
Hence, when he said 'I purple you army', he meant 'I love you to infinity army'. He explained it as:
Do you know what does color purple means? Purple is the last color of the rainbow colors, so it means I will trust and love you for a long time.
He additionally said: I wish I can see you for a long time, just like the Borahae means. We’ll always trust you and go up the stairs with you.
Since then, ARMYs around the world uses purple as a symbol of love. And every mention of the phrase by BTS meant them thanking army for their support and love.

Contribution of BTS to the world:


The BTS members were active in a number of charitable groups.
They collaborated with UNICEF to establish the Love Myself campaign in 2017, which generated funds to support initiatives aimed at preventing violence against children and teenagers.
In light of the COVID-19 epidemic, BTS urged people to get immunised and urged young people around the world to be a spark for change.
To discuss the increasing spike in violence against Asian Americans in the US, the band met with US President Joe Biden in 2022.

Contribution of BTS to South Korea:

The economy of South Korea has grown by around $5 billion year, or 0.5%, as a result of rising interest in Korean literature, music, and films as well as in Korean language education.
According to a 2018 survey, during the previous four years, South Korea had welcomed 800,000 foreign tourists annually for BTS-related travel.

Contribution of army:

ARMYs act to change the world in response to BTS's socially conscious lyrics. As a result, the fandom frequently engages in activism for charity organisations and socially conscious concerns including the COVID-19 epidemic, racial discrimination, children's rights, and refugee crises.

About songs and awards:

  • Mic Drop(2017): the first Korean group to be certified Gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA)
  • Love Yourself: Tear (2018): first from South Korea to top the Billboard 200
  • Love Yourself: Answer (2018): the first Korean album to receive RIAA Platinum certification
  • Dynamite(2020): first all-South Korean act to reach number one on both the Billboard Hot 100 and Billboard Global 200
  • They became the quickest artist to achieve four US number-one singles since Justin Timberlake in 2006 owing to the release of their follow-up hits "Savage Love," "Life Goes On," "Butter," and "Permission to Dance."

Current Status: Military Service

According to hybe, members will be joining South Korean military, which by law needs every capable man to join military for nearly two years (Men usually needs to enroll to serve army by age of 28, but according to a bill passed in 2020, pop stars and other cultural artists may delay till age of 30). According to the plan, members may re-unite by 2025.
